Cyborg farmers: Embodied understandings of precision agriculture

Daniel van der Velden, Laurens Klerkx, Joost Dessein, Lies Debruyne

全文概要总结

作为一位专注于农业技术社会学与后人文主义理论的学者，我认为本文对精准农业领域做出了重要且及时的理论贡献。它超越了当前普遍存在的“技术乌托邦”与“技术敌托邦”的二元对立论述，通过引入“赛博格农人”（cyborg farmer）这一概念，深刻揭示了农人在实际操作中如何将数字技术与其身体经验、感官知觉和地方性知识进行复杂而动态的整合。

1. 论点 (Argument)

本文的核心论点是，精准农业并非简单地用机器取代农人或使其与土地疏离，而是促使农人演变为“赛博格农人”。这种新型农人将精准技术内化为自身感官与身体的延伸，在一个由数据驱动的理性与基于身体的直觉知识构成的“富有成效的张力”（productive tension）中运作。这种整合过程不仅没有削弱农人的主体性，反而通过校准和融合不同形式的知识，维持了他们对农场决策的控制力，从而抵制了纯粹算法理性的统治。

2. 论据 (Evidence)

论据主要来源于对荷兰25位作物种植者、承包商、研究人员和农业科技开发者的深度访谈。访谈的质性数据，特别是农人的直接引述，构成了强有力的经验支撑。例如，农人描述如何利用NDVI（归一化植被指数）地图来指导他们的田间巡视（“evening walk”），将数字信息作为一种“增强视觉”，引导他们去物理地触摸、观察和感受作物；他们通过回忆多年前埋设管道的经历来解读土壤图上的异常数据；他们强调必须“拿着土钻下地”来验证传感器数据的真实性。这些生动的例子证明了数据与身体经验之间的持续对话与校准。

3. 论证过程 (Reasoning Process)

作者首先批判性地梳理了关于精准农业的两种主流论述——技术乐观主义与社会学批判。随后，文章构建了一个基于后人文主义、新唯物主义和“超越表征”（more-than-representational）理论的分析框架。该框架将农人的身体置于知识生产的中心，将技术视为身体的延伸，而非对立物。通过这一理论透镜，作者分析了访谈数据，论证了农人的知识并非被数据所取代，而是与数据形成了一个动态的“集结体”（assemblage）。数据（如地图、数值）与身体感知（看、闻、触摸）和历史记忆相互印证、相互校准。这种将经验证据与前沿社会理论相结合的论证方式，使得“赛博格农人”这一概念从一个抽象隐喻转变为一个具有坚实经验基础的分析工具。

4. 方法 (Method)

研究采用了定性的研究方法，核心是半结构化访谈。通过目的性抽样和滚雪球抽样，选取了25位在精准农业领域具有实际经验的关键行动者，确保了数据的相关性和深度。数据分析过程结合了归纳式编码和理论驱动编码，先从数据中提炼出初步类别，再运用“身体化知识”等核心理论概念进行深入分析。作者坦诚地指出了访谈方法在捕捉“超越表征”的身体经验方面的局限性，但认为通过受访者的语言，依然可以“一窥”他们的实践世界。

5. 案例 (Case/Example)

研究的核心案例是荷兰的作物种植者群体。这个案例具有代表性，因为荷兰农业技术水平高，精准农业的应用相对广泛。具体的微观案例贯穿全文，如：一位农夫将肥料撒播过程视为一次高效的田间巡视，因为拖拉机的高度和速度提供了独特的观察视角；另一位农夫通过对比多年的记忆来判断田地中的“湿点”，并以此来验证或质疑传感器数据；还有一位农夫在畅想未来时，甚至提出将芯片植入员工体内以让计算机读取他们的“想法”，这极端地说明了身体经验在未来全自动化农业构想中依然是不可或缺的核心。

INTRODUCTION

CONCEPTUALISING THE CYBORG FARMER

本章详细阐述了“赛博格农人”的理论基础。它将“身体化知识”（embodied knowledge）定义为核心概念，强调知识是通过身体的、感官的、超越纯粹语言表征的实践而产生的。作者借鉴了关系-物质转向和后人文主义思想，认为知识是在人类、非人类（包括技术）和环境的集结体中共同生成的。技术不再被视为外在工具，而是被整合进扩展的自我，成为身体的一部分，共同塑造着农人的感知和认知方式，最终形成了农人-技术-环境的混合体——赛博格农人。

  • 提出本文使用的“身体化知识”术语，强调知识的物质性（corporeal）和超越表征（more-than-representational）的本质。
    In this article, we use the term embodied knowledge, which stresses the corporeal aspect of knowledge and highlights the more-than-representational nature of this knowledge (Maclaren, 2019; Phillips, 2014).
  • 解释了超越表征的知识与日常生活经验、惯例和身体体验紧密相连，无法被文本完全捕捉。
    Morethan-representational knowledge can be understood as attuned to the lived experience where the representation of this knowledge, such as in this text, is never able to fully represent this knowledge, as this knowledge is based on everyday experience, routines, encounters and embodied experience (Carolan, 2008; Lorimer, 2005).
  • 采用关系性视角，认为知识是通过扩展的自我，在人与非人行动者的集结体中产生的。
    This means that we see knowledge as a relational achievement that is produced through the body, meaning that knowledge is produced through the extended self, in an assemblage of human and non-human actors (Brians, 2011; Clark, 2008; Gieser, 2008; Heersmink, 2012).
  • 批判心物二元论，认为知识并非仅在大脑中产生，而是根植于身体在世界中的行动和体验。
    The mind should not be seen as a separate or single organ, where knowledge is produced based on inputs, but rather as an element in the production of knowledge, where knowledge is produced through the lived body, which simultaneously experiences, acts in and is aware of the world (Clark, 2008; Cutler & MacKenzie, 2011).
  • 引用Carolan的研究，说明拖拉机等传统技术如何通过提供独特的动感体验和视角，塑造农人的身体和认知方式。
    This is, for example, linked to the use of the tractor by farmers, which provides a unique kinaesthetic experience and viewpoint, placing the farmer inside the field and allowing them to see and feel the land through the tractor (Carolan, 2008).
  • 使用梅洛-庞蒂（Merleau-Ponty）的“盲人手杖”比喻，阐释技术如何成为身体的延伸，扩展感官的范围。
    A further example of embodiment of technology is provided by Merleau-Ponty (2002), who described the blind man’s stick, which: ‘ceased to be an object for him, and is no longer perceived for itself; its point has become an area of sensitivity, extending the scope and active radius of touch, and providing a parallel to sight’ (Merleau-Ponty, 2002, p. 165).
  • 引用Krzywoszynska对葡萄园护理的研究，说明身体化知识如何在农业实践中具体体现，如通过味觉、嗅觉、触觉来决定修剪哪根枝条。
    It is a knowledge developed in the agro-ecological context of the farm, developed and shaped through lived experience. To understand embodied knowledge is to understand the impact of taste, smell, feelings, sounds and views in agriculture on the production of knowledge. This embodied knowledge determines which branch is pruned in a vineyard (Krzywoszynska, 2016)...
  • 进一步发展Lundström和Lindblom的观点，认为技术不仅是辅助知识生产，而是与人融为一体，共同构建身体化知识的赛博格。
    We take this a step further, where we do not see technology merely contributing to knowledge production. Instead, an integration of human and technology, a cyborg, constructs and informs embodied knowledge (Heersmink, 2012).
  • 借鉴后人文主义思想（如Haraway），将赛博格农人定义为人类与非人类行动者（技术）的混合体或杂合体。
    This follows broader posthumanist approaches, which have set out the intertwined nature of humans and technology, where these collectives are hybrids or cyborgs of nature and technology (Bear & Holloway, 2019; Finstad et al., 2021; Haraway, 2006).
  • 最终定义赛博格农人：一个通过技术得到扩展和增强，但仍然在农业生态空间中保持身体在场的、人类与非人类行动者的身体化集体。
    The farmer becomes a cyborg through the extension and augmentation with technology but remains present and embodied in an agro-ecological space.

INTEGRATING AND VALIDATING PRECISION AGRICULTURE WITH EMBODIED KNOWLEDGE

本章是研究的实证核心，展示了荷兰农人如何将精准技术与身体化知识进行整合与验证。通过丰富的访谈引述，作者揭示了几个关键实践：1）农人将传感器（如摄像头、NDVI）视为视觉的延伸，能够“看到”肉眼不可见的作物问题；2）他们通过叠加个人历史记忆和实地经验（如记得哪里挖过管道、哪里是湿地）来解读和验证数据图谱，使抽象数据变得有意义；3）数据往往是触发行动的起点，促使农人亲自下地勘察，通过触摸土壤、观察作物来确认问题根源；4）农人强调长期积累的“农夫智慧”和“感觉”在决策中的重要性，抵制盲目遵从数据或算法。同时，本章也揭示了数据驱动方法与身体化方法之间的张力，一些农人和技术开发者追求更科学、算法化的决策，而另一些则强调保留农人的直觉和自主权。

  • 阐述了传感器技术如何增强农人的视觉，使其能够更早、更精确地发现作物问题，这是一种身体能力的增强。
    With cameras, we can look much deeper and far better. You can anticipate [issues such as nutrient deficiencies, diseases, etc.] (Farmer CF5)
  • 举例说明了农人如何利用其个人历史和实地经验来解读看似客观的数据图。没有这些背景知识，数据对外部人来说是无意义的。
    So you can recognise some things in the map, like this spot is a wet spot where they had to dig a pipe through the land. [...] Look, you even get to see the temperature of the grain. (Farmer CF12)
  • 描述了NDVI数据如何引导农人进行有目的性的田间巡查，将数字洞察与物理世界的感官经验（如在撒肥车上观察）结合起来。
    I now go through all the parcels by NDVI values. That means I already know where I need to go in the field... Because with spreading fertiliser... with the height you have, you can see every bit of the field. (Farmer CF4)
  • 强调农人坚持亲自下地的重要性，认为计算机无法替代对土地湿度的实际感受和现场观察，这是对纯粹远程办公式农业的否定。
    I do not see how it would work if I sit here in the kitchen and I send the tractor into the land and that’s that. Because you have certain spots that you know when you prepare the land, it could be a wet spot, you don’t see that in the computer. (Farmer CF11)
  • 突出了长期经验（“22个季节”）和“农夫智慧”在决策中的核心作用，农人以此来判断和校准数据，而非盲从。
    I am not someone who blindly follows the numbers, but I use my own knowledge and insights. And maybe I’m wrong, but I have been farming for 22 seasons now. So I think I can sense which way it goes and connect the dots. (Farmer CF7)
  • 揭示了数据驱动思维与身体化知识之间的张力。一些研究者认为精准农业的精髓在于用“数字”取代“感觉”来进行决策。
    So you learn to see your crops and soils in a different way. You learn to count the numbers and to base your decision-making on those numbers. And that is a different mentality than waking up in the morning and deciding how you’ll farm that day. (RES2)
  • 展示了部分农人对缺乏“科学”算法的焦虑，他们希望决策能有更客观的依据，而不仅仅是“凭感觉”。
    You have a soil scan, organic matter levels, and you want to do something, but which algorithms to use? And then I play it by ear, but that is not scientifically proven. (Farmer CF14)
  • 引用业内人士的观点，强调成功的技术必须与农人的“感觉”相结合，技术是辅助工具，不能替代好的农人。
    They want to keep the feeling for it. To retain the insight, it’s very important to preserve that... Because in the end the basis is agronomy and that remains. We only deliver a tool that can aid the farmer. (IND2)
  • 通过一个未来主义的场景，一位农夫强调即使是完全自动化的系统，也需要接入他和他员工的“思想”和经验，决策权最终不能是“黑箱”。
    Who decides when to spray? Not the computer... I would have to be connected to a plug and you’d have to implant my employees with a chip. So the computer can read their minds and see what they think, and translate that to tasks. It will not be a black box. (Farmer CF2)

THE CYBORG FARMER—MIND, BODY AND TOOL ON INTIMATE TERMS

本章为讨论部分，对前述实证发现进行理论升华。作者重申，“赛博格农人”是在数据、技术、农人知识和农业生态环境的集体中运作的。这种身体化的技术使用方式，打破了心/物二元论，将农人理解为既思考又感知的整体。研究发现，数据驱动的知识文化与身体化的知识文化之间存在一种“富有成效的张力”，赛博格农人正是在这种张力中进行校准和决策，从而保留了自身的主体性。这挑战了那些认为精准农业会导致“算法锁定”或算法理性霸权的观点。最后，作者将“赛博格农人”与van der Ploeg的“虚拟农人”进行对比，并提出未来可能存在不同的“数字耕作风格”（digital farming styles）。

  • 总结了赛博格农人如何将数据解读与田间的多元感官体验相结合，通过扩展的身体来发展知识。
    Knowledge develops through the body, extended through technologies and situated in a broader space/environment. This conceptualisation responds to questions on how precision agriculture is changing farms, farmers and farm work and how knowledge is mobilised (Klerkx, 2021; Klerkx et al., 2019; Prause, 2021; Rotz et al., 2019b).
  • 引用Klauser和Pauschinger关于无人机的研究，说明精准技术如何为农业开辟新的维度（如“立体农业”），增强人类感官。
    This extension is reminiscent of the ideas of Klauser and Pauschinger (2021) who describe how drones open up the air as a new dimension in agriculture (‘volumetric agriculture’) and connect to ideas on emerging forms of ‘augmented agriculture’ (Klerkx, 2021) in which technology enhances human senses.
  • 证实并扩展了早期研究（Legun等；Lundström & Lindblom）的发现，即农人知识在理解和应用新技术（如机器人、决策支持系统）中至关重要。
    With our findings, we confirm the earlier work of Legun et al. (2022) and Lundström and Lindblom (2018), who show the importance of farmers’ knowledge in relation to autonomous robots on farms and for decision support systems, respectively.
  • 提出身体化思维为农村社会学提供了一种超越心/物二元论的有效路径，将农人视为一个思考与感知并存的主体。
    This provides a way to go beyond mind–body dichotomies (Darnhofer, 2020), in which the cognitive is not understood as primary, as emotions and the materiality of the body are taken seriously, that is, the farmer is understood as a being who thinks and senses.
  • 回应Tsouvalis等人早期关于精准农业中知识文化冲突的研究，认为这种冲突并非完全负面，而是一种“富有成效的张力”。
    At the start of the century, Tsouvalis et al. (2000) wrote about the tension between knowledge cultures in precision agriculture... we set out how the tension between knowledge cultures is productive.
  • 通过强调农人主体性，本文的发现对那些认为精准农业将导致算法锁定或算法理性支配的观点（Carolan, Gardezi & Stock, Miles）提出了修正。
    This nuances authors who describe the potential of precision agriculture as an algorithmic lock-in or as a domination of algorithmic rationality over other forms of knowledge (Carolan, 2020b; Gardezi & Stock, 2021; Miles, 2019).
  • 将“赛博格农人”与van der Ploeg的“虚拟农人”概念进行对比，认为赛博格农人代表了地方性农耕技艺在面对专家和数据驱动系统时的坚韧存在。
    In a sense, the cyborg farmer is also a play on the virtual farmer described by van der Ploeg (2003). The cyborg farmer is a persistence of the local art of farming in the face of the virtual farmer constructed by the expert, sensor and data-driven knowledge system...
  • 基于农人与技术结合程度的不同，提出未来研究可以探索“数字耕作风格”这一新概念，呼应了耕作风格研究的传统。
    What our findings add to this earlier work is that the different ways of engaging with precision technologies and the intensity of being a cyborg farmer could lead to what has been coined as ‘digital farming styles’ (Klerkx et al., 2019). ...a new avenue in a long tradition of farming styles research in rural sociology (e.g., van der Ploeg, 1994, 2012; Vanclay et al., 2006)
