Farmers who tinker: Grounded alternatives to incrementalism and the growth imperative

Matt Comi PhD

全文概要总结

作为一名长期关注农业社会学与科技研究（STS）交叉领域的学者，我对本文的分析如下。Comi博士的这篇文章，是对当前北美农业中占主导地位的“增长命令”（growth imperative）和技术官僚式“渐进主义”（incrementalism）路径提出的一项精妙而及时的批判。通过对美国啤酒花（hop）种植业的民族志考察，作者引入并阐释了“修补”（tinkering）这一核心概念，将其定位为一种根植于实践、具有能动性且充满关怀（care）的社会-物质互动模式。

1. 论点 (Argument)

本文的核心论点是，在由大规模、高资本、垂直整合的“新种植园模式”主导的农业格局中，一部分中小型农场主通过“修补”——即对投入品、基础设施、植物材料乃至市场关系进行即时性、情境化和创造性的改造——开辟出了一条不依赖规模扩张的生存与发展路径。这种“修补”实践不仅是一种经济策略，更是一种重构农场社会-物质关系（socio-material relations）的本体论政治（ontological politics），为实现更具韧性和公正性的农业可持续转型提供了区别于主流“渐进主义”的、更具草根性的替代方案。

2. 论据 (Evidence)

论据主要来源于对22位美国啤酒花种植者的混合定性研究，尤其侧重于其中9位在规模、地区或方法上偏离主流模式的农场主。具体的经验证据分为三类：1）基础设施的修补，如农场主自行设计和建造啤酒花采摘机以规避高昂的设备成本；2）植物材料与市场关系的修补，如将公共品种的啤酒花通过本地化命名（“Kanook”）进行品牌重塑，或借鉴葡萄酒庄园的“风土”（terroir）概念来构建与本地酿酒师的独特关系；3）知识与生态实践的修补，如采用“树液分析”（sap analysis）替代传统的叶柄分析，作为向再生农业（regenerative agriculture）转型过程中更具响应性的田间决策工具。

3. 论证过程 (Reasoning Process)

作者的论证逻辑层次清晰。首先，他在理论层面建构“修补”概念，将其与STS领域的“组合体”（assemblage）理论和关怀伦理（ethics of care）相结合，明确其“即兴、在地、具体、微小”的特质，并将其与目的驱动、自上而下的技术创新模式（如生态现代主义）和保守的“渐进主义”区分开来。其次，通过对啤酒花产业背景的描绘，作者揭示了大型农场通过垂直整合和市场营销手段所强化的“增长命令”。随后，文章进入经验分析部分，通过三个层层递进的案例（技术、产品、生态），具体展示“修补”如何在实践中运作，如何重塑人、植物、技术和市场之间的关系。最后，在讨论部分，作者将“修补”的经验发现重新与理论对话，强调其作为一种“实验性”路径，能够重塑整个农业实践的内在逻辑，而非仅仅在现有框架内做边际调整，从而为农业可持续转型政策提供了重要的启示。

4. 方法 (Method)

研究采用了混合定性方法，包括对16名参与者的农场进行实地访谈和观察，以及因疫情原因对另外6名参与者进行的远程访谈。这种方法使研究能够深入捕捉农场实践的物质细节和农场主的决策逻辑。作者明确指出，其研究路径是探索性的，遵循约翰·劳（John Law）“收集而非分类”（gathering over sorting）的原则，旨在呈现社会-物质安排的多样性，将研究发现视为“经验性启发”（empirical provocations）而非可供普遍化的实证证明。这一方法论立场与文章所探讨的“修补”实践的异质性、情境性特质高度契合。

5. 案例 (Case/Example)

核心案例是美国啤酒花种植产业，这是一个经历了从传统小农商品生产向大规模、高度自动化和垂直整合模式快速转型的行业。这一背景为研究“另类”实践提供了绝佳的对照组。文中的具体案例极富说服力：Patrick自制价值1500美元的啤酒花采摘机，挑战了动辄上万美元的工业设备；Wade将堪萨斯州种植的Chinook啤酒花重新命名为“Kanook”，创造性地构建了地方风土价值；Susan的农场采用树液分析法，更动态、精确地理解植物的营养需求，并以此为基础推动再生农业实践。这些案例生动地诠释了“修补”如何将物质限制转化为创造性机遇，从而在主流模式之外开辟出独特的生存空间。

BACKGROUND
  • 定义“修补”及其与“关怀”的联系: “修补”被定义为一种直接、社会化的物质世界介入，旨在为修补者带来更好的结果。它被描述为一种即兴、在地、具体且通常是小规模的实践，本质上是一种“关怀”（care）行为。
    Tinkering is a direct, social engagement in the material world to produce more augmented outcomes for the tinkerer. Tinkering is also an act of care (Mol et al., 2010).
  • 修补的社会-物质与关系性维度: 修补涉及对即时技术和安排的社会与物质双重干预。这个过程是关系性的，涉及到农场中的各种行动者（包括人类和非人类），是一种协作性的关怀。
    Tinkering involves paired social and material interventions into the immediate technologies and arrangements with whom stakeholders (both humans and nonhumans) interact (Donati, 2019; Higgins et al., 2023). ... This socio-material arrangement of care is the subject of widening discourse in agri-food studies on how care in agriculture settings (re)produces new outcomes and possibilities (Alarcon et al., 2020; Stock, 2016).
  • 对“关怀”概念的批判性审视: 关怀并非无条件的政治善事，它可能限制接受者的权力。因此，本文讨论的是农业物质关系背景下的“协作性关怀”。
    Care, notably, is not an unfettered political good... care can ‘render a receiver powerless or otherwise limit their power’ (Martin et al., 2015, p. 627).
  • 将“修补”置于“组合体”理论框架下: “修补”被置于社会-物质组合体（socio-material assemblages）的理论框架中。当农民进行修补时，他们是在重塑其特定农业组合体中的物质行动者和关系。
    This study nests the concept of tinkering within the broader question of how socio-material assemblages of agriculture may be engaged with by people (e.g., farmers) in ways that do (or do not) support more sustainable outcomes (Bronson & Knezevic, 2016; Carolan, 2010; Comi, 2020a). ... Assemblages are sets of socio-material relationships that reproduce outcomes—they are flat and messy hierarchies that eschew traditional social science categorical domains (Deleuze & Guattari, 1987; Müller, 2015; Müller & Schurr, 2016).
  • 农民作为重构农业组合体的行动者: 广泛的文献支持农民作为多样化社会-物质景观中的行动者，他们通过（重新）安排农业组合体来创造新的结果、可能性和未来。
    A range of literature examines farmers as actors in diverse socio-material landscapes who are engaged in (re)arranging assemblages of agriculture to produce new outcomes, possibilities and futures (Carolan, 2008; Darnhofer, 2020; Goodman, 2001).
  • “修补”与农民创新研究的对话: “修补”推进了关于农民发明家和创新者的研究，这些研究强调科学界和农业企业常忽视农民创新的开放精神。
    Tinkering as applied to farmer-innovators also pushes forward a small but important thread of literature on farmer-inventors and farmer-innovators... scientific communities and agri-business interests have often ignored the innovations and open-source ethos of many farmer-inventors (Carolan, 2017b; O’Flynn et al., 2018; Shutes, 2003).
  • “修补”与新自由主义及增长命令的对立: “修补”的安排与新自由主义模式形成对比，后者强调商品化和无休止的经济扩张与物质榨取。
    The arrangement of tinkering contrasts with the material/social/economic arrangements indicative of neoliberalism. The latter flexibly take up materials, commodifies those materials and translates their value into a range of social worlds (Carolan, 2017a; Centeno & Cohen, 2012; Stock et al., 2014). It is well known that as with capitalist structures, this neoliberal model results in a growth imperative that emphasises unsustainable economic expansion and material extraction (Moore, 2017).
  • “修补”与生态现代主义的区别: 与生态现代主义者设想的自上而下、技术官僚式的创新不同，“修补”展示了草根创新如何成为实现更公正未来的工具之一。
    This differentiation from a technocratic capital-focused approach to innovative systems changes sets tinkering approaches apart from the more top-down biological innovations imagined by eco-modernists over the last three decades (Asafu-Adjaye et al., 2015; Spaargaren, 1997).
  • “修补”在可持续转型话语中的定位: 将“修补”置于关于可持续未来的广泛讨论中，包括对增长命令的批判、农业生态学、去增长等。它被视为一种嵌入关怀的物质干预，对于想象可持续的粮食生产转型至关重要。
    Troubling the growth imperative, production treadmills and seeking alternatives have been a core consideration in rural as well as environmental social science for the last 40 years (Alexander, 2020; Bronson, 2019; Buttel, 2016; Catton, 1980; Gould et al., 2016; Spaargaren, 1997)... Stock and Szrot (2020) make the case that just food transitions, as a function of justice, are socio-politically bound up in care, whether conceptualised as stewardship or through directive action...

FINDINGS: Tinkering on a tiny farm

本节展示了小农场主如何通过“修补”基础设施和工具来应对高昂的成本，从而获得更大的财务自主权。这是一种对物质世界的即兴、协作式改造，以解决眼前的问题。

  • DIY解决方案应对高成本: 小农场主通过自己动手（DIY）的方式来解决昂贵的基础设施问题。例如，Patrick因无力承担新的或二手的啤酒花采摘机，选择自己动手建造了一台，成本远低于市场价。
    Patrick, for example, is starting a small two-acre operation in Eastern Washington... Instead, he opted to build his own picker (see Figure 1)—an approach he has used with much of his infrastructure from his micro-kiln to his home-modified pelletiser and harvesting stand.
  • 就地取材与资源再利用: 其他农场主，如Hannah和Jason，则通过砍伐自家的木材制作棚架，而不是购买昂贵的电线杆，以此来降低启动成本。
    Tinkering may also involve the repurposing of other goods—as with Patrick’s operation, startup costs are a large inhibiting factor for other small growers; Hannah and Jason address some of this startup cost issue by forgoing telephone pole trellising and logging their own trellis (see Figure 2).
  • 修补作为维持生计的策略（而非主业）: 对于这些小农场主而言，啤酒花种植通常不是主要收入来源，而更像是一种退休爱好或辅助性收入。这使得他们的经营具有一定的时间限制和风险。
    For all three of these growers, one key commonality is that the hop field is not currently their primary source of income... Patrick notes that if they do not turn a profit in 5 years, he plans to quit.
  • 通过“小”来提供灵活的服务: 这些小型种植者将自己的规模劣势转化为服务优势，为当地小型酿酒厂提供高度响应和定制化的服务，满足了那些无法与大型供应商签订直接合同的酿酒厂的需求。
    By staying small, these growers aspire to offer a more flexible kind of growing arrangement... One of the brewers said he ordered something, he was going to Yakima to pick it up. And they called him when he was driving there and said, ‘We don’t have your hop anymore. We have this one if you want that’. (Hanna)

FINDINGS: Tinkering with what you grow

本节探讨了中小型农场主如何通过“修补”他们种植的植物材料以及与市场的关系来创造竞争优势。这涉及到对野生品种的识别、重新想象和品牌化，是一种人与植物协作的修补关系。

  • 通过品牌重塑创造独特性: 面对大型农场的竞争，小农场主通过品牌化来突显其产品的地域独特性。例如，Wade将堪萨斯种植的Chinook品种重新命名为“Kanook”，以传达其独特的风土特征。
    So, the Kanook rebranding, which people do the same thing in Michigan, they call it Michigan Chinook or whatever, but for us, the biggest issue was not wanting to grow something and then delivering it to a brewer thinking that they were going to get traditional Chinook, so we just put a little twist on it just to kind of display that it’s a little bit different when grown here in Kansas. (Wade)
  • 与植物和环境的互动探索: Wade的农场还尝试种植了其他独特的公共品种，如Southern Brewer和野生neomexicanus。这个过程充满了试错，区域性的环境差异既带来了机遇也带来了挑战。
    From an agronomic standpoint, they were really hard to grow for us, so ended up pulling those out as well. [Wade]
  • 借鉴“风土”（Terroir）概念重塑价值: Hannah和Jason试图借鉴葡萄酒行业的“风土”概念，强调其产地独特的土壤条件，希望以此改变啤酒花市场过度追求新奇性的趋势，转向对地域和一致性的价值认可。
    Everybody touts the grapes and the terroir where they’re growing this stuff. So I’ve read a couple articles on... People have written about the hops in terroir, and so we’re hoping we get to see a little different results from our area than other people do. (Hannah)
  • 创新市场关系模式: 他们还借鉴葡萄酒庄园的做法，让当地酿酒厂“认领”一排啤酒花，以此建立更紧密、更具参与感的合作关系，从而重塑市场关系和消费者价值评估。
    Well, and it’s similar in the vineyards, the wineries will have their name on a row... And they can come out anytime and check on them (Jason)

FINDINGS: Tinkering on medium and large farms

本节论证了“修补”并非小农场的专属。中型和大型农场也通过借鉴基于关怀的模式，重构社会-物质关系，以实现财务和环境的可持续性。核心案例是采用“树液分析”作为向再生农业转型的工具。

  • 采用新知识工具——树液分析: 一些中大型农场主正在尝试用树液分析（sap analysis）取代行业标准的叶柄取样（petiole sampling）。他们认为树液分析能更准确、动态地反映植物内部的营养流动，从而做出更即时的农事决策。
    Industry norms for plant health testing involve petiole sampling; however, these growers identify jokingly as being part of a ‘secret club’ of growers who believe that sap analysis provides a more accurate sampling of what is actually moving in the plant... (George)
  • 树液分析作为一种关怀植物的实践: 这种方法被视为一种更能理解和尊重植物作为生命有机体动态“植物性”（plantiness）的方式，体现了对非人类行动者的关怀。
    To borrow a term, sap analysis could be described as an approach that cares about hopbine’s plantiness (Head & Atchison, 2016; Head et al., 2014).
  • 修补作为向再生农业转型的路径: 对于Susan经营的农场来说，采用树液分析是其向再生农业（regenerative agriculture）更广泛转型的一部分。这一转型强调“更好地耕作”和长期可持续性，而非短期增长。
    Notably, this approach emphasises farming better, with long-term sustainability in mind and diverse profit-making as an outcome instead of short-term growth as an immediate goal.
  • 转型需要分布式行动者网络: 向再生农业的转型是一项复杂的任务，需要一个由顾问、公司和知识网络组成的分布式行动者网络的支持。这表明“修补”在规模扩大时，其复杂性也随之增加，但同时也创造了新的合作机会。
    Starting cover-cropping, switching to organics for pest control and fertiliser or producing grazing plans is a complex task on any size farm, and it requires a distributed network of actors to enable this kind of ‘tinkering’ approach.
  • 修补作为一种“工具箱”方法: Susan将她们的实践描述为一种“工具箱里的工具”，连接了历史实践（如放牧）和现代科技，强调了农业实践中即兴发挥的空间。这与大型农场采用的自上而下、基于审计的系统性可持续性方法（如GreenChief项目）形成鲜明对比。
    And now we just have a few different tools in the toolbox than what maybe prior operators had because of science. We’ve had advances in technology that my grandpa never would’ve dreamed that we could figure this stuff out. (Susan)

DISCUSSION & CONCLUSION
  • “修补”与“渐进主义”的根本区别: 讨论部分的核心在于区分“修补”和传统的“渐进主义”。尽管两者都可能带来更可持续的结果，但“修补”是一种根本性的实践重定向，它通过直接、实验性的方式改变组合体内部行动者的关系。而“渐进主义”（如此处以GreenChief项目为例）则是在不改变核心实践的前提下，对实践的边缘进行限制或调整。
    However, the key difference between these two examples is that tinkering represents a small shift that reorients the farming practices in total. It is direct and experimental... This differs from the incrementalist approach, which sets limits or otherwise addresses the margins of practice but leaves the farming practice in total largely intact.
  • “修补”作为通往“后增长”农业的路径: “修补”为从传统农业向去增长（de-growth）模式（如农业生态学、再生农业）的转型提供了一种可行的、阶梯式的路径。它通过“从小处着想”的日常关怀和实验，而非宏大的顶层设计，来实现转型。
    ...this study sheds light on how tinkering provides an alternative model to incrementalism as a pathway for stepwise transitions towards models of agriculture outside the growth imperative.
  • 对技术解决方案的批判性反思: “修补”的视角解释了为何许多大规模的“技术修复”（techno-fixes）方案常常会固化而非解决农业问题。
    It also shows how technology change can be part of sustainable transitions while providing an explanation for why many large-scale techno-fixes often reify problems in agriculture instead of solving these problems (Fairbairn & Guthman, 2020; Guthman & Zurawski, 2020).
  • 结论与未来研究方向: 结论强调，“修补”并不必然带来公正或可持续的结果，但其面向关系的实验性特质能产生更灵活、响应性和适应性强的成果。政策层面应支持农民的实验，为他们提供创新和试错的安全网。未来的社会科学研究，特别是采用物质性或关系性方法的，应关注参与者如何与其工作的组合体互动，并进一步将能动性（intentionality）等概念融入组合体研究中。
    Beyond pushing forward disciplinary framings for appropriate social science considerations, this work highlights alternative ways scholarship on alternatives and sustainable transitions may handle multiplicity and grassroots change in agricultural praxis.
