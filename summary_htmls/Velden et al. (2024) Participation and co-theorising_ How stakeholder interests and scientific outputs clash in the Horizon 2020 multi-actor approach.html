Participation and co-theorising: How stakeholder interests and scientiﬁc outputs clash in the Horizon 2020 multi-actor approach

Daniel van der Velden, Lies Debruyne, Laurens Klerkx & Joost Dessein

全文概要总结

1. 论点 (Argument)

本文的核心论点在于，欧盟“地平线2020”计划所倡导的多行动者方法（Multi-Actor Approach, MAA）在实践中存在一种深刻的内在冲突。这种冲突表现在：一方面，项目追求让利益相关者（stakeholders）深度参与，以产生具有实践相关性的知识；另一方面，学术研究本身要求理论建构和科学产出。作者指出，为了维持利益相关者的参与热情和确保研究的实践价值，研究人员会有意地将“共同理论化”（co-theorising）——即邀请利益相关者参与理论概念发展和科学产出——排除在参与过程之外。这种策略性选择虽然保障了项目的短期运行，却牺牲了参与式研究更深层次的变革潜力。最终，文章认为这一困境并非研究人员的个人选择所致，而是“地平线”项目框架的结构性问题：其要求在项目申请阶段就预设理论框架，从而从根本上限制了与利益相关者共同发展理论的空间。

2. 论据 (Evidence)

该研究的主要论据来源于对一个“地平线2020”研究与创新行动项目（H2020 DESIRA）的深入案例分析。研究团队对该项目中负责领导7个“生活实验室”（Living Labs）的17名研究人员进行了三轮半结构化访谈。这些访谈的直接引文构成了核心证据，揭示了研究人员对于参与式研究的理想化期望、在实践中遭遇的困境，以及他们为解决这些困境所采取的具体策略。例如，受访者普遍表达了对让利益相关者参与理论建构的认同，但同时又担心这样做会因其“过于学术”而吓跑参与者，导致他们退出项目。

3. 论证过程 (Reasoning Process)

文章的论证逻辑层层递进。首先，它从宏观层面描绘了当代科学研究向参与式、跨学科模式（即知识共同创造）转型的背景，并引入了“共同理论化”作为深度参与的理想形态。其次，通过访谈数据，文章展示了研究人员在实践中面临的三重张力：项目预设目标、利益相关者的实际需求以及研究人员自身的学术追求之间的矛盾。关键的论证环节在于揭示研究人员如何“解决”这一张力——他们采取了“分离策略”（separation），即在参与活动中将满足项目硬性要求的任务与满足利益相关者实际需求的部分分离开来，前者被高效完成，后者则被视为“真正的”参与。这种分离恰恰将理论探讨和科学产出过程隔离在利益相关者之外。最后，文章将这一现象从个体行为层面提升至结构性批判，指出“地平线”项目的资金申请和管理机制是导致这种“浅层参与”的根本原因。

4. 方法 (Method)

本文采用的是定性案例研究方法，并带有些许行动研究的色彩。研究对象是H2020 DESIRA项目中的七个“生活实验室”，这些实验室分布在欧洲不同国家，聚焦于农业、林业和农村地区的数字化问题。数据收集的核心手段是与领导这些实验室的研究人员进行17次半结构化访谈。值得注意的是，该研究具有自我反思性（self-reflexive），因为前两位作者本身也负责其中一个实验室，并对自己进行了同样的访谈。数据分析采用了扎根理论的方法，通过Nvivo14软件进行开放编码和主题编码，从而系统地识别出研究人员在参与式研究中面临的核心张力和应对策略。

5. 案例 (Case/Example)

具体的案例是H2020 DESIRA项目，该项目旨在评估数字化对欧洲农业、林业和农村地区社会经济的影响。项目通过在20个国家设立“生活实验室”来践行多行动者方法。文章聚焦于其中七个实验室（位于荷兰、拉脱维亚、意大利、西班牙、法国、苏格兰和比利时）。一个典型的例子是，研究人员提到，尽管项目的宏大目标是探讨“数字化”的影响，但当地的利益相关者（如农民）可能更关心社区建设、市场价格或具体的生产问题。为了留住他们，研究人员不得不把项目要求的“数字化”议题作为一项任务快速完成，然后将大部分时间用于讨论利益相关者真正关心的话题，这使得共同探讨“数字化”的理论框架变得不切实际。

Introduction

本章节介绍了研究背景，指出在解决“重大挑战”的背景下，知识的共同创造和利益相关者的参与已成为科学研究，特别是欧盟资助项目中的主流范式。作者追溯了这一趋势至更广泛的科学民主化和从线性知识生产模式向跨学科、应用导向模式的转变。然而，文章迅速指出了其中的争议：参与的深度和形式各不相同，尤其在“地平线2020”这类多行动者项目中，参与往往是事件驱动和浅层的。核心问题被提出：这种参与能否超越数据收集，延伸至理论发展和科学产出的共同创造，即“共同理论化”（co-theorising）。作者明确了本文的研究空白，即鲜有研究探讨研究人员如何处理在共同理论化过程中出现的各种张力，并由此引出研究问题。

  • 知识共同创造作为解决复杂问题的关键： 作者指出，知识和创新的共同创造被视为解决复杂或“棘手”问题的核心。
    The cocreation of knowledge and innovations is often described as a key aspect of solving complex, or “wicked” problems (Hakkarainen et al., 2022).
  • 整合多样化知识的系统性努力： 知识共同创造是整合不同知识体系以应对“重大挑战”的广泛努力的一部分。
    Such knowledge co-creation is part of a multifaceted yet cohesive effort to integrate diverse knowledges and integrated approaches in knowledge development, aimed at addressing the “grand challenges” of our time (Caniglia et al., 2020).
  • 从线性到跨学科的知识生产转型： 这一转变被描述为从学科导向、线性和等级化的知识生产模式，转向社会分布、跨学科和应用导向的模式。
    It is commonly described as a move away from disciplinary, linear and hierarchical modes of knowledge production to a socially distributed, transdisciplinary and application-oriented mode of knowledge production (Jahn et al., 2012; Nowotny, 2003).
  • 科学民主化与多方参与： 这种转型呼应了更广泛的科学民主化呼声，让创新成为多方行动者共享的实践。
    This resonates with a broader call for democratising science, where innovation becomes a shared practice between multiple actors and stakeholders, involving their concerns, knowledge, experiences and practices (Ingram et al., 2020).
  • 农业科学中参与式研究的渊源： 农业科学领域对多样化知识的包容自80年代末以来日益普及，常以Chambers的作品为起点。
    This development has seen increased popularity in agricultural science since at least the late 80s with Chambers (1989) frequently cited as the starting point of a broader involvement of diverse knowledges in agricultural research (Neef & Neubert, 2011).
  • 关于参与潜力和程度的不同观点： 学界对参与式研究的潜力和所需参与程度存在不同看法。
    However, there are varying views on the potential of participatory research and the required extent of participation (Basco-Carrera et al., 2017; Doudaki & Carpentier, 2021).
  • 关于应达到的参与水平的持续辩论： 对于应达到何种参与水平，存在反复的批判和辩论。
    There are also recurring critiques and debates over what level of participation should be reached (Mobjörk, 2010).
  • 参与阶梯理论的应用： Arnstein的参与阶梯理论被用来区分不同层次的参与，从非参与到完全的公民控制。
    The levels or intensity of citizen and stakeholder participation desired, or required, have been recognized in the participation ladder (Arnstein, 1969). This highlights the different forms that participation can take, from non-participation and manipulation, through consultation, to full citizen control and partnership (Arnstein, 1969; Galende Sánchez & Sorman, 2021).
  • 欧盟研究项目中多行动者方法的定位： 多行动者方法是欧洲研究资助背景下的一个组成部分，鼓励项目包含多样化的行动者以共同创造知识。
    Multi-actor approaches fit within a broader European research funding context, where research projects are encouraged, if not required, to include diverse actors in the process of co-creating demand-focused knowledge (Slavova et al., 2023).
  • 欧盟项目中参与的事件驱动特性： 对这些项目中参与的描述指出，其通常以短期事件为中心。
    Considering the intensity and depth of participation, Feo et al. (2022) describe participation in these projects as centred around events, with short moments of participation.
  • 对参与形式和成果的根本性辩论： 尽管学者们讨论了吸纳多样化知识的价值，但对于这种多样性对参与过程意味着什么，观点不一。
    On a more fundamental level, there are running debates about the forms and desired outcomes of participation. While authors discuss the value of involving a diversity of knowledges, there are different views on what this diversity means for the participatory process (Doudaki & Carpentier, 2021; Dunlap et al., 2021; Koskinen, 2014).
  • 多行动者项目的普遍做法： 常见做法是认为多样化知识能加强科学知识、使其产出合法化并提高实践应用性。
    The common approach in multi-actor research projects is that a diversity of knowledges is valuable in that it can strengthen scientific knowledge and legitimise the outputs of the research process, and increase its applicability in practice (Adamsone-Fiskovica & Grivins, 2022).
  • 对浅层参与的批判： 这种做法被批评为将参与局限于研究方法和分析工具层面，而未能从根本上质疑项目背后的概念和理论。
    This has however been critiqued as limiting participation to involvement in research methods and analytical tools, as it does not fundamentally question concepts and theories underpinning the projects (Felt et al., 2012; Hakkarainen et al., 2022).
  • 呼吁更深度的参与——共同理论化： 作为回应，有学者呼吁让利益相关者参与科学知识和产出的生产，如科学文章和理论发展。
    In response, other authors have called for the involvement of stakeholders in the production of scientific knowledge and outputs such as scientific articles and the development or advancement of scientific theory (Arribas Lozano, 2018; Caretta & Pérez, 2019).
  • 超越“地平线2020”项目的常规参与形式： 这种深度参与超越了“地平线2020”项目的普遍做法，后者的参与通常止步于数据收集和成果实施。
    This goes beyond the forms of participation common to Horizon 2020 research projects, where participation usually does not extend beyond participation in data gathering and implementation of project results in practice (Caretta & Pérez, 2019; Feo et al., 2022).
  • 研究的核心关注点： 本研究关注的核心是参与的期望、参与者的贡献以及研究人员如何处理期望与项目需求之间的张力。
    These tensions between what participation is desired, what contributions participants can make and how researchers deal with tensions between desired participation and other needs of the research project are the principal interests of this study (Schikowitz, 2020).
  • 研究的创新贡献： 文章指出，鲜有研究分析研究人员如何处理参与式研究带来的张力，特别是关于通过整合多样化知识来发展理论概念的方面，本文旨在填补这一空白。
    While there are a good number of case studies focused on how participatory research is done, and how it can be improved, there are relatively few studies that analyse how researchers deal with the tensions that participatory research brings, especially concerning the development of theoretical concepts and broader theories through integrating diverse knowledges (Felt et al., 2016; Schikowitz, 2020; Slavova et al., 2023), and this is the gap the study addresses and where the study makes a novel contribution.

Theoretical framework

本章构建了研究的理论框架，分为三个部分。第一部分（2.1）探讨了参与的层次性，强调参与并非非黑即白，而是一个谱系，并指出即便是较低层次的参与也可能是有意义的，关键在于能否让利益相关者感到被倾听并能影响研究。第二部分（2.2）深入分析了参与式研究中固有的张力，特别是科学知识生产和社会相关知识需求之间的冲突。作者引用文献指出，这种张力常常导致项目回归传统科学产出，牺牲了社会相关性。同时，也探讨了理论本身对参与者的潜在隔阂感，并引出“好理论”应为所有参与者提供可用工具的观点。第三部分（2.3）聚焦于“共同理论化”的具体概念，将其定义为让利益相关者参与科学产出（如论文、报告）的生产过程，并强调这是一种包含“认知责任”（epistemic accountability）的协作学习过程，能够增强研究的有效性和反思性。

  • 参与形式的多样性： 鉴于“参与式研究”涵盖多种方法，需要明确本文所研究的参与形式。
    Considering the participation ladder, and the diversity of approaches that are classified as forms of participatory research (Fleming et al., 2021), there is a need to clarify and specify the forms of participation studied in this article.
  • 本文研究的参与形式界定： 本文中的参与是一种研究方法，利益相关者被邀请提供和生产知识，但并未参与所有研究阶段。
    To clarify participatory methods in this case study, the participation we speak of in our research was a form of participation as a research method, where stakeholders were engaged to provide and produce knowledge, but where they were not involved in all phases of the research process. (Caretta & Pérez, 2019)
  • 不完美但仍有意义的参与： 这种参与模式被一些学者视为不完美，因为它未赋予利益相关者在研究前期和决策中的影响力。
    To some authors this is already an imperfect participatory process, as the stakeholders cannot influence the research process beforehand and are also not involved in broader decision-making (Basco-Carrera et al., 2017; Jahn et al., 2012; Renn, 2021).
  • 参与的谱系而非等级： 参与并非只有达到完全公民控制才有意义；即便是较低阶梯的参与也可能是有价值的。
    However, this is not to say that participation is only meaningful when stakeholders have decision-making powers in the research project. Several authors indicate that meaningful participation can occur even in processes that at first sight seem to be low on the participation ladder (Neef & Neubert, 2011).
  • 研究项目是不同模式的混合体： 研究项目往往是线性、科学驱动和跨学科、应用导向这两种模式的混合体，而非处于谱系的某一极端。
    To place research at either end of this spectrum is too general and simplistic, as research projects often contain elements of both models, where it is more accurate to speak of a blend between these archetypes (Ingram et al., 2018; Neef & Neubert, 2011).
  • 有意义参与的核心标准： 有意义的参与需要为利益相关者提供被倾听的权利和影响研究目标及成果的可能性。
    Rather, participation needs to be meaningful to stakeholders, through providing them a right to be heard and a possibility to contribute to setting research objectives and steering research outcomes (Nesheim et al., 2021).
  • 参与中的目标冲突： 增加利益相关者的话语权可能与生产科学知识的目标或其他项目要求相冲突。
    Providing meaningful participation through involving stakeholders in decision-making can however be in tension with other objectives of a participatory research project (Schikowitz, 2020). Increased stakeholder say can be in tension with the production of scientific knowledge, and funder requirements do not always allow stakeholders to be involved in setting out the project goals and aims (Feo et al., 2022; Fieldsend et al., 2021).
  • 科学知识与社会相关知识的内在张力： 参与式研究的核心张力在于生产科学知识与构建社会相关知识之间的矛盾。
    There is an inherent tension between the production of scientific knowledge and the socially relevant knowledge that is expected to be built in the process of participatory research (Brandt et al., 2013; Felt et al., 2016; Schikowitz, 2020).
  • 科学标准的规训作用： 参与过程中产生的经验和知识需要被转化为符合科学标准的形式，这本身就存在张力。
    Experiences, knowledge and reflections are translated into a scientific form that is valued following scientific standards (Felt et al., 2016; Schikowitz, 2020).
  • 参与式研究的多重张力： Felt等人描述了参与式研究面临的多种张力，如在追求科学合法性与变革之间、普适性与地方性之间、以及科学与社会界限之间的矛盾。
    Felt et al. (2016) for example describe several tensions: the promise of radical change to science while retaining legitimacy within science, tensions between generalisation and local relevance, and between developing broad knowledge and expertise while retaining distinctions between science and society.
  • 回归传统科学产出的倾向： 在张力之下，开放知识生产的尝试往往会退回到传统的科学成果上，科学相关性压倒了社会相关性。
    In light of the aforementioned tensions, initiatives for opening up knowledge production often fall back on rather conventional scientific outcomes. Scientific relevance prevails over societal relevance, and limits the potential of participatory research. (Schikowitz, 2020)
  • 将社会相关性置于首位的观点： 作为回应，一些学者认为科学产出在参与式研究中应是次要目标，优先考虑社会相关性和利益相关者利益。
    In response, several authors take the view that scientific outputs might need to become a secondary aim in participatory research, as societal relevance and stakeholder interests need to be given priority (Brandt et al., 2013; Schikowitz, 2020).
  • 对科学理论的矛盾态度： 科学理论有时被视为对参与者无用，只在特定学术圈内有意义。
    Scientific theories are sometimes described as having no real use to participants in the process, lacking utility and only finding meaning within specific networks of shared understandings (Gergen & Gergen, 2008). Other authors share the view that stakeholders might not find meaning in the scientific process of developing theories (Djenontin & Meadow, 2018).
  • 理论的风险与必要性： 过于宽泛的理论不符合地方现实，而完全放弃理论又会失去知识整合的机会。
    The risk is that overly broad and general theories do not fit the complex realities on the ground, while foregoing theories completely risks losing out on a consolidation of knowledge that emerges from the involvement of broader actors in research processes (Schlüter et al., 2022).
  • “好理论”的标准： “好理论”应为实践者、学者和其他行动者提供易于理解和使用的工具，帮助共同创造共享知识。
    Friedman and Rogers (2009) set out that “good theory” provides accessible and useful tools for practitioners, academics, and other actors/participants alike.
  • 理论讨论的赋能作用： 讨论理论可以帮助研究参与者和研究人员达成共识，更好地理解他们的社会现实，并让地方知识参与理论建构。
    Discussing theory can then provide a way for research participants and researchers alike to come to a shared understanding and to understand their social reality better (Genat, 2009). ... to enable research participants to interpret the world in new ways, to understand their social reality and to have local knowledge participate in theory-building (Friedman & Rogers, 2009; Málovics et al., 2021).
  • 社会稳健性知识的理想状态： 在理想的参与式研究中，通过利益相关者的参与，生产出的知识对科学和社会双方都具有相关性，从而减少了两者之间的张力。
    This connects to the idea of socially robust knowledge that we set out, where socially relevant scientific knowledge is developed through the participation of stakeholders (Nowotny, 2003).
  • 共同理论化作为协作学习过程： 涉及更多行动者的理论化过程变成了一个更具协作性和审议性的学习过程。
    Schlüter et al. (2022) mention that by involving more actors, such as in a multi-actor process, theorising becomes a more collaborative and deliberative learning process that involves a wider diversity of actors.
  • 共同理论化与认知责任： 共同理论化的概念包含对参与行动者的“认知责任”（epistemic accountability）。
    Interesting in this light is the notion of cotheorising (which includes a level of epistemic accountability to the different actors involved in the participatory process) (Caretta & Pérez, 2019; Huebner et al., 2017).
  • 研究者在参与式研究中的嵌入角色： 在参与式研究中，研究者通常是嵌入在他们所观察的系统之中，而非外部观察者。
    The role of the researcher in theorising can be distinguished between outside observers or as embedded in the system they seek to observe, with the latter being more common for participatory research (Schlüter et al., 2022).
  • 共同理论化的不同形式： 共同理论化可以采取不同形式，核心是研究者对被研究社群负责，从简单的结果验证到深度参与分析和产出。
    Depending on the community involved in the participatory process, this might involve simply verifying the results but can also include a deeper involvement of the researched community in the analysis and production of scientific outputs (Caretta & Pérez, 2019).
  • 共同理论化对研究有效性的贡献： 在科学产出中使用参与式方法可以提高研究的整体有效性，允许参与者对研究者的分析提出异议。
    Participatory methodologies in the production of scientific outputs can improve the overall study validity (Caretta & Pérez, 2019).
  • 共同理论化的价值： 这种做法能丰富知识生产过程，增强反思性，去中心化研究者的角色，并让利益相关者的知识在科学知识生产中发挥更大作用。
    It allows for increased reflection, allows for a de-centring of the role of the researcher and allows for a greater role of stakeholder knowledges in the production of scientific knowledge (Arribas Lozano, 2018).

Methods

本章详细阐述了研究方法。首先（3.1），介绍了案例研究的方法，明确了研究对象是H2020 DESIRA项目，一个旨在研究数字化对农业、林业和农村地区社会经济影响的“研究与创新行动”（RIA）项目。该项目通过在欧洲多国设立20个“生活实验室”（Living Labs）来实施多行动者方法。作者界定了生活实验室作为以用户为中心的开放式创新过程，并指出了该项目中参与活动的局限性（限于焦点小组和工作坊，利益相关者未参与项目早期设计）。具体描述了工作坊中使用的两个框架：一个用于评估数字化当前影响，另一个（STEEP框架）用于探讨未来情景。其次（3.2），介绍了具体的研究方法。作者将研究过程定位为一种行动研究，旨在发展项目理论概念的同时研究参与过程本身。数据主要通过对7个生活实验室的研究人员进行的三轮共17次半结构化访谈获得。研究包括自我反思环节，因为前两位作者也主持了一个生活实验室。数据分析使用Nvivo14软件，结合了归纳式和演绎式编码。

  • 生活实验室作为用户中心创新过程： 生活实验室被定义为以用户为中心、具有显著共同创新的开放式创新过程。
    Living labs are used to describe usercentred, open innovation processes with significant co-innovation (Gamache et al., 2020).
  • 生活实验室的在地化特征： 生活实验室的特点是其基于特定地点和“现实生活”的共同创造过程，尽管这并非其独有。
    Particular to living labs is the place-based and “real-life focus” of these co-creation processes, focusing on a particular community or group of users, although this does not need to be exclusive to living labs (Toffolini et al., 2021).
  • 成熟社区参与的潜在影响： 一些生活实验室建立在长期运行的社区之上，这可能增加社区在参与过程中的话语权。
    Several of the living labs were part of longer-running initiatives and communities. Other research has pointed out that this can lead the community to have an increased say in the process of participation (Felt et al., 2016).
  • 项目结构对参与的限制： 尽管如此，这些社区也未必参与了项目的其他阶段，如研究提案的撰写或成果评估。
    However, these communities were also not necessarily involved in other phases of the project, such as developing the research proposal or evaluating the resulting knowledge (Jahn et al., 2012).
  • 评估数字化影响的框架： 在第一个工作坊中，使用了一个特定框架来引导利益相关者讨论数字化在他们具体情境中的正负面影响。
    For this workshop a specific framework (described in Metta et al., 222) was used in the workshop to have stakeholders discuss the positive and negative impacts of digitalisation in their specific contexts.
  • 未来情景分析的框架： 在后续工作坊中，使用了另一个框架（STEEP框架）来讨论数字化的未来情景。
    Following this first workshop, a second and third workshop was held to discuss potential future scenarios for digitalisation, using the framework described in Duckett et al. (2022). This framework focuses on Societal, Technological, Economic, Environmental and Political (STEEP) drivers of change (Hunt et al., 2012).
  • 行动研究作为方法论定位： 研究过程被视为一种行动研究，旨在协同解决问题的同时产生新知识。
    We approached our research process as a form of action research, aiming to develop a process that would build on the theoretical concepts developed in the project while also studying the process of participation itself. This follows the view of action research as collaborative problem-solving while also aiming to generate new knowledge (Coghlan, 2011).
  • 自我反思在研究中的作用： 对作者自己主持的生活实验室也使用同样的访谈问题，这被视为提高研究者反思性的一种方式。
    The same interview questions used for the other interviews were used for these reflective interviews, following the view that this improves the reflexivity of us as researchers (Olmos-Vega et al., 2023).

4.1. Tensions in extending participation (Findings)

本节展示了研究人员对扩展参与的矛盾心态。一方面，受访者普遍认同参与应该被扩展，甚至延伸到让利益相关者共同建构和批判科学理论的“共同理论化”层面。他们认为这能让理论更真实、更有用。然而，另一方面，他们也指出现有参与形式的局限性，如参与活动仅限于少数几次工作坊，间隔时间长，导致利益相关者难以持续投入。更重要的是，研究人员普遍存在一种“恐惧”，担心引入“学术性”或理论性的讨论会让利益相关者感到无聊、觉得无用，从而失去兴趣并停止参与。他们强调，必须尊重利益相关者的时间，确保活动对他们有直接价值。因此，尽管研究人员在理念上支持共同理论化，但在实践中，他们为了维持参与而主动排除了这类活动。

  • 对更深层次参与的向往： 研究人员表达了希望让利益相关者参与科学理论发展的愿望，认为这能使理论更真实、更有用。
    I think we are moving towards a more responsible way of how that knowledge is produced anyway, which is exactly what you are doing here. [...] it could pull a few things apart, but make those theories a bit more authentic and useful in the real world as well. Hopefully. (R3Scot)
  • 对参与式理论的信念： 受访者坚信，通过集体行动和参与式方法来发展理论是解决问题和应用研究的强大途径。
    I am convinced that collective action is the way to solve problems and to connect people. [...] but participatory theory is a great and powerful way to work and to apply research. That is for sure. (R3France)
  • 参与的间断性和局限性： 研究人员指出，项目中的参与活动往往是孤立的事件，间隔时间长，利益相关者无法跟进中间的研究过程，也意识不到理论部分的存在。
    So if we think that we had three workshops in three years, let’s say, they also forget what we did last time and it’s too far away. We do all this work in the middle but they don’t follow that. [...] I am not sure if they connected the information and they were, because they are not aware of the theory part. (R3Italy)
  • 维持参与的挑战： 研究人员描述了维持利益相关者兴趣的困难，需要不断激励他们，并承认在将高阶学术概念转化为对参与者有价值的内容时存在挑战。
    And this is not the first time where we see this challenge, where really you are struggling to retain the interest of your [...] participants. You are trying to put concepts that are very highly academic in nature, well to translate them to participants, and then to translate them back. (R1Lat)
  • 对利益相关者时间的尊重： 研究人员强调要尊重利益相关者的时间，因为他们参与工作坊几乎没有直接回报，因此避免讨论他们认为无用的理论概念。
    We tend to be very respectful with the time of stakeholders because normally they do not win anything by coming to our workshops, or very little. Right? (R1Spain)
  • 害怕用学术术语“累”到参与者： 研究人员担心，用学术术语和理论讨论会让参与者感到疲惫，并质疑这对他们第二天的工作有何用处，从而导致他们退出。
    So you don’t want to tire people with [academic] terminology. Because they will wonder what use it is to them when they return to work the next day. (R2NL)
  • 对共同理论化实际效果的怀疑： 研究人员认为，让对理论一无所知的利益相关者参与讨论，可能得不到多少有用的反馈，反而会给他们增加负担。
    You do the workshops to provide maximal value to your participants... you do not want to burden them, to make them think about these concepts and to ask for their opinion. [...] And I think to myself, how much useful feedback would you get? With people who don’t know anything about these theories. (R1Belgium)

4.2. Tensions in the participatory research project (Findings)

本节揭示了参与式研究项目中更深层次的结构性张力。核心问题在于，泛欧项目的统一目标与各个生活实验室的地方现实之间存在脱节。项目要求所有参与方都围绕一个共同主题（如“数字化”）展开讨论，以获得可比较的跨国研究结果。然而，访谈数据显示，这个预设的主题对许多地方的利益相关者来说并非首要关切。他们可能更关心社区建设、市场价格或其他更紧迫的本地问题。这导致研究人员必须不断地将讨论“拉回”到项目主题上，从而在项目要求与利益相关者的真实兴趣之间制造了一种持续的紧张关系。这种不匹配使得共同理论化的前景更加渺茫，因为如果连基本主题都缺乏共鸣，深入探讨其背后的理论概念就更不可能了。研究人员因此不得不在满足项目要求和为参与者提供有意义的体验之间进行艰难的平衡。

  • 项目主题与地方现实的错位： 研究人员指出，项目的核心关注点（数字化）在地方层面并非最重要的议题；社区建设等社会性议题更为关键。
    See, the point is [...] the game changers here are on the social, on community building. [...] and the discussion is not about the game changers, but it’s about how to create a community that is motivated, where producers and consumers are part of a shared system. (R2NL)
  • 研究人员需“强行”引导话题： 利益相关者有许多更紧迫的关切，如支付能力、饮食结构变化等。研究人员承认，他们不得不持续地将对话引导回“数字化”这一项目规定的主题上。
    That is a tricky thing, because well, I could say it was very central, but then I have to keep in mind that we were constantly pushing them back to talk about digital... So I would not say that digitalisation is on top of their mind. And that is quite interesting. (R2Lat)
  • 预设理论框架的普适性与局限性： 为了实现跨案例比较，项目需要使用普适的理论概念。然而，这些概念并非为特定地方情境量身定做，导致其在本地应用中显得格格不入。
    To seek an overarching theory that can be used by all living labs requires theoretical concepts that can be used in a variety of contexts and across different socio-technical circumstances. The concepts are not adapted to the local context and local needs, their fit needs to be universal, rather than specific for each of the cases.
  • 在项目需求和参与者需求间寻求平衡： 研究人员在访谈中表达了他们的两难处境：一方面要运用项目概念来获得对项目有用的结果，另一方面又要避免在工作坊中直接提出这些概念，以免破坏对话，并要确保参与者能有所收获。
    So you are constantly balancing between getting results that are useful to the project and on the other hand providing something meaningful to the people who give their time to you. (R2NL)

4.3. Further tensions and strategies to make participation happen (Findings)

本节详细阐述了研究人员为应对前述张力所采取的具体策略，并引入了第三重张力：参与活动与产出科学成果之间的矛盾。研究人员明确指出，若要严格遵循科学方法论，就会限制工作坊的参与潜力。为了让参与对利益相关者“有用”，他们采取了一种“分离策略”：首先，完成项目所必需的、但对利益相关者可能无趣的“任务”（如填写问卷、讨论特定概念）；然后，转向利益相关者真正关心的、更具实践性的、甚至可以“发牢骚”的环节。这些“发牢骚”的环节对科学研究本身可能没有直接价值，但对于建立信任和维持参与至关重要。这种策略的核心思想是，将从参与者那里“获取信息”的过程变得尽可能高效，以减少对他们的“打扰”，从而为更具地方相关性的活动留出时间。最终，这种妥协导致了参与过程与理论建构、科学产出过程的实际分离。知识的提炼和转化为科学成果，仍然是研究人员在参与活动之外独立完成的工作。

  • 科学贡献与参与实用性的冲突： 研究人员认为，若要追求科学贡献，就需要更严格地控制工作坊流程，但这会损害其实用性。因此，他们选择在完成项目任务后，转向那些对科学不那么有趣但对参与者重要的话题。
    If you would focus on a scientific contribution you would need to be much stricter with how to do the workshops. To keep it methodologically sound. [...] But if you [...] want it to be useful, well we finally said, we do what we need to do for [the project], but we add other elements. [...] the practical questions that are not scientifically interesting. (R3NL)
  • 学术讨论对参与者的疏离效应： 当讨论过于学术化时，研究人员能观察到参与者明显失去兴趣（“眼睛开始呆滞”），这提醒他们必须将讨论拉回到更实际的层面。
    Then you can sort of see their eyes glazing over. We’re going too far in this academic direction now and we have to remember not to do that too much. (R3Scot)
  • “给予回报”作为参与策略： 对于已经多次参与研究的社群，研究人员将当前项目的活动视为一种“回馈”的机会，主动将活动重点从理论探讨转向解决当地的实际问题。
    So what we decided to do now [...] was that this was a chance to give something back. To connect to the questions that are there and couple those to the project needs. So yeah, we could have chosen to go more theory-heavy, but we actually went the opposite way. (R3NL)
  • 信息“高效提取”策略： 研究人员将自己的出发点描述为如何以最高效的方式从参与者那里获得所需信息，其动机是尽可能少地占用他们宝贵的时间。
    So we do work from a perspective of what we need to take from them, from the group, how can we do that as efficiently as possible. (R3Belgium)
  • 妥协的结果：分离： 最终，为了平衡各方需求，研究人员将参与活动与理论概念、项目任务分离开来。知识的生产发生了，但将其提炼为具体产出的过程仍然是研究人员的专属领域，因为这些产出被认为对利益相关者价值不大。
    The reality then is, in our project, that tension is resolved by separation. Participation happens but is separated from theoretical concepts and from project activities. Knowledge production happens, but the refinement of the knowledge into concrete outputs is the domain of the researcher.

5.1. Fear of stakeholders ending their participation (Discussion and conclusion)

本节讨论了研究结果的一个核心发现：研究人员普遍担心利益相关者会终止参与。作者分析认为，这种担心源于项目实践中存在的根本张力——利益相关者的实践需求、项目成果要求和科学产出之间的矛盾。为了化解这种张力，研究人员谨慎地围绕利益相关者的兴趣来设计活动。他们认为，将利益相关者纳入理论探讨会牺牲活动的实践相关性，因此选择将理论和概念性讨论排除在外。这种做法虽然保障了参与的持续性，却牺牲了“共同理论化”的潜力。作者进一步追问，为何“共同理论化”被视为与利益相关者无关？答案在于项目结构的缺陷：在项目提案阶段就已确定的、旨在实现跨欧洲普适性的宏大主题（如数字化），在被带到具体的地方情境时，往往与当地的实际需求脱节。这种普适性要求与地方特殊性需求之间的矛盾，呼应了现有文献对参与式研究中普遍性与地方性知识冲突的讨论。

  • 为维持参与而牺牲理论探讨： 研究人员之所以担心利益相关者退出，是因为他们认为引入理论探讨会降低活动的实践相关性。为保障实践性，他们选择将理论讨论排除在生活实验室之外。
    The researchers’ fear of stakeholders ending their participation indicates that a focus on including the stakeholders in theorising was seen to come at a cost of practical relevance to the stakeholder. To safeguard the practical relevance of participation, researchers choose to exclude discussion on theory and theoretical concepts from the living lab. (following Schikowitz, 2020)
  • 项目预设主题与地方现实的脱节： “共同理论化”被视为无关紧要，一个重要原因是项目在提案阶段就已确定的宏大主题，在具体的地方情境中可能并不重要。
    As seen in several of the living labs, the overarching project aims did not always fit the context of the stakeholders, as happens when topics related to digital impacts defined at the proposal stage of the project are brought to a local context where these topics are not relevant.
  • 普遍性成果与地方性知识的张力： 项目需要产出普遍适用、可跨欧洲比较的结果，但这与地方知识的特殊性和具体需求产生了冲突。
    At the same time, the project needs universal, cross-European results, despite the realisation that these results might not be universally true across living labs, situated in different countries and contexts (echoing Klerkx et al., 2017). This is in agreement with the tensions described by Felt et al. (2016), where generalisable results are demanded despite the realisation that locally specific knowledge is needed (echoing Berthet et al., 2016).

5.2. Separation rather than furthering participation (Discussion and conclusion)

本节进一步分析了研究人员应对张力的核心策略——“分离”，并将其置于更广阔的理论背景中进行讨论。作者指出，与一些文献认为研究人员在面临冲突时会优先考虑科学相关性的观点不同，本研究的发现更为复杂：研究人员会根据情况调整策略，有时甚至会优先考虑利益相关者的需求，而非科学产出或项目要求。他们采取“分离”策略，即将满足项目硬性要求的活动（被视为对利益相关者的疏离）最小化，以便为“真正的”参与（满足利益相关者的实际需求）腾出空间。这种做法被作者解读为一种在“参与阶梯”上动态上下移动的行为，而非停留在某一固定层次。这揭示了多行动者项目运作中的一种常态，即通过不断的妥协来维持项目运转。然而，这种妥协的代价是，共同理论化和科学产出过程被视为对参与的阻碍，而非其有机组成部分，从而固化了知识生产中研究者与参与者之间的壁垒。

  • 对科学知识生产的批判性视角： 参与常常被用作一个标签，但实际上多样化的知识仍被视为待收集的数据，用于传统的科学知识生产。
    Participation is often a name put on processes where diverse knowledges are still seen as data to be collected, to be used in the production of scientific knowledge (Boogaard, 2021; Latulippe & Klenk, 2020).
  • 对“科学优先”假设的修正： 与一些研究发现相反，本研究中的研究人员并未必然将科学相关性置于其他形式的相关性之上。
    Interestingly, our findings indicate that researchers in this research did not necessarily fall back on scientific relevance over other forms of relevance in response to these tensions, which is contrary to some of the findings of other authors (Felt et al., 2016; Schikowitz, 2020).
  • 多行动者项目中的冲突与妥协： 本研究的发现与Slavova等人的观点一致，即多行动者项目中项目需求与利益相关者需求会发生冲突，而研究人员通过不断妥协来维持项目运作。
    Slavova et al. (2023) analysed multi-actor projects specifically and indicated the tensions in these projects, where project and stakeholder needs can clash. [...] This fits with the analysis of Slavova et al. (2023) that compromises are constantly made to keep the multi-actor project functioning.
  • “分离”作为核心策略： 研究人员采取的策略是，将那些被认为会疏远利益相关者的活动（如满足项目要求、产出科学成果）的时间最小化，以便让“真正的”参与得以进行。
    We observed an approach that several researchers took, to limit the time spent on aspects that were seen to alienate stakeholders. This is especially true for project requirements such as scientific outputs, where stakeholder participation was minimised to achieve the needed results.
  • 在“参与阶梯”上动态移动： 研究人员在项目中并非固定在某一参与层次，而是根据不同活动和目标，在“参与阶梯”上灵活地“上升和下降”。
    Researchers thus “ascend and descend the participation ladder” during the project; there is no specific level of participation that is achieved throughout the project, but rather varying levels of participation depending on the activity and the aims of the researcher (Arnstein, 1969; Galende Sánchez & Sorman, 2021).

5.3. Implications for future participation and co-theorising (Discussion and conclusion)

本节总结了研究的启示，并对未来参与式研究提出了建议。作者首先指出，本研究发现的挑战在一定程度上是“地平线2020”项目结构特有的，例如项目时间有限、参与仅限于特定事件。在此背景下，生活实验室的功能更像是传统的利益相关者咨询活动，而非真正的共同实验空间。作者反思，如果共同理论化的练习与利益相关者更直接相关的产出（如基于他们开发的未来情景的出版物）相结合，或者如果项目主题本身就是由利益相关者共同设定的，那么共同理论化可能会更成功。由此，作者得出一个关键结论：深化参与的关键在于项目起始阶段，而非后期的数据收集或理论建构阶段。应在项目设计之初就让利益相关者参与进来。然而，“地平线”项目的结构（包括其后续的“地平线欧洲”计划）——时间紧迫、合作伙伴众多、且在提案阶段就固化了框架——使得这种早期参与难以实现。最后，作者呼吁对当前项目中这种演绎式的理论应用方法提出质疑，建议未来的项目可以采取更归纳的方法，即基于不同国家和情境中参与式研究的实际成果来发展项目理论，这样可能更好地与利益相关者的需求相结合。

  • 项目结构的局限性： 研究中发现的挑战与“地平线2020”项目为期四年、参与活动仅限于事件的结构有关。
    This, to an extent, is specific to the structure of the investigated research project, which is limited in time and scale to four years with participation being limited to events (Feo et al., 2022).
  • 生活实验室的实际功能： 在本研究中，生活实验室的功能更像是传统的利益相关者咨询，而非真正的共同实验空间。
    It can also be questioned whether the living labs in this research were used as co-experimental spaces, which would set them apart from earlier participatory exercises (Potters et al., 2022; Toffolini et al., 2021).
  • 共同理论化成功的潜在条件： 如果项目主题与利益相关者的兴趣更紧密地结合（最好是由他们共同设定），共同理论化可能会更成功。
    There is the possibility that co-theorising would be of more interest to stakeholders when the living lab topic is more closely aligned with their interests (as would be the case when the stakeholders are directly involved in setting this topic) (echoing Numans et al., 2019).
  • “地平线”项目框架的结构性障碍： “地平线2020”及其后续的“地平线欧洲”项目结构，本身就限制了共同理论化的可能性。
    This is a limitation of the project structure of the Horizon 2020 projects in question as well as its successor Horizon Europe (see also Shortall and Meredith, 2025).
  • 早期参与的重要性： 深化参与最有效的方式是在项目设计阶段就让利益相关者介入，而不是在后期的数据收集或共同理论化阶段。
    Before deepening participation in the data gathering or in co-theorising, it makes more sense to include stakeholders in the project design (following Caretta & Pérez, 2019).
  • 现有项目框架的制约： 当前的项目形式，因其时间限制、伙伴众多和结构固化，使得早期参与难以实现。
    The question that remains is whether the research project in its current form allows for this approach to joint theory building, as these projects are restricted in time, deal with a large number of partners and actors, and their structure does not easily allow participation at early stages of the project (as also observed by Cronin et al., 2022; Klerkx et al., 2017; Shortall and Meredith, 2025).
  • 对演绎式研究范式的批判与建议： 作者质疑在项目开始时就预设一个理论框架的演绎式方法，并建议采取更归纳的方法，从不同地方的参与式研究成果中发展理论。
    Our research calls into question this rather deductive approach, as there is limited connection to the reality in the living labs. An achievable step towards creating the potential for co-theorising might be to develop project theories more inductively based on the outcomes of the participatory research in different countries and contexts...
