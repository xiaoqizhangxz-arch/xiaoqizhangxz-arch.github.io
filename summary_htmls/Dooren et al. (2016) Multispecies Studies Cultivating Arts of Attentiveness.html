Multispecies Studies Cultivating Arts of Attentiveness

THOM VAN DOOREN, EBEN KIRKSEY, and URSULA MÜNSTER

全文概要总结

1. 论点 (Argument)

作为环境人文学科中一个关键的新兴领域，多物种研究（Multispecies Studies）的核心论点在于，它倡导并实践一种“关注的艺术”（arts of attentiveness）。这种艺术要求我们超越传统的人类中心主义视角，通过“充满激情的沉浸”（passionate immersion）深入理解并回应人类与非人类（包括动物、植物、微生物乃至“无生命”之物）之间复杂、动态且相互构成的纠缠关系。此文认为，这种关注不仅是一种认识论上的转向（epistemological shift），更是一种本体论（ontological）、政治和伦理上的实践。它旨在挑战既有的物种、能动性（agency）和生命等概念，并最终致力于在一个生态日益“被摧毁的景观”（blasted landscapes）中，培养更具责任感、更能促进共同繁荣的共存方式或“世界构建”（worldmaking）。

2. 论据 (Evidence)

本文作为一篇纲领性的导言，其论据并非来自单一的实证研究，而是通过对一个广阔且跨学科的知识谱系进行综合与重构来支撑其论点。作者们征引了（1）生态学与哲学奠基人（如Gregory Bateson的“有机体-在-其-环境中”）；（2）当代关键理论家（如Isabelle Stengers的“相互捕获”，Donna Haraway的“伴侣物种”与“cobecoming”，Anna Tsing的“跨物种关系”，Karen Barad的“能动实在论”）；（3）来自原住民思想的悠久关系性思维传统；（4）对生物学和动物行为学等自然科学文献的批判性重读；（5）艺术与表演实践作为另类的知识生产方式；（6）以及领域内学者们具体的民族志研究案例（如对病毒、真菌、昆虫、农场动物的研究）。这些多元的证据共同构建了多物种研究的合法性、丰富性及其智识根基。

3. 论证过程 (Reasoning Process)

文章的论证逻辑是层层递进的。首先，它以“生命在多物种社群中涌现”这一本体论前提出发，确立了“共同生成”（cobecoming）和“纠缠”（entanglement）为分析基石。其次，通过与“动物研究”等相关领域进行比较，界定了“多物种研究”的独特性，即其更广阔的分类学视野和对“复数性”（multiplicity）的强调。接着，文章系统阐述了该领域的核心方法论——“充满激情的沉浸”，并将其细分为与科学家、原住民、艺术家等不同知识主体的合作与互动。随后，论证进入了更深的哲学层面，探讨这种方法论转向如何引发本体论和认识论的重塑，即“世界构建”（worlding）的概念，打破了主客体、自然与文化的二元对立。最后，文章将这一系列理论探讨导向其最终的伦理与政治关切：在一个权力关系不平等的纠缠世界里，我们如何承担责任，如何培养“关注的艺术”以实现更公正的共同生活。

4. 方法 (Method)

本文本身采用的是理论综述（theoretical review）和学科导论（programmatic introduction）的方法，旨在为该特刊乃至整个领域绘制一幅知识地图。然而，文章的核心在于阐述并倡导多物种研究所采用的一系列“沉浸式方法”（immersive methods）。这些方法包括：多物种民族志（multispecies ethnography）、动物行为-民族志（etho-ethnology）、对科学文献的批判性解读、与科学家和原住民知识持有者的合作研究、艺术实践与表演性干预（performative interventions）、以及对日常伴侣物种的准民族志式观察（para-ethnography）。这些方法共同的特点是强调研究者的具身参与、情感投入以及对非人类主体经验世界的尊重与好奇。

5. 案例 (Case/Example)

由于本文的综述性质，它并未深入剖析单一案例，而是通过一系列简短的案例点缀其论证，以展示多物种研究的广度与活力。这些案例包括：花与蜜蜂的协同进化关系；挑战人类中心分类学的蚂蚁（根据原住民知识，蚂蚁也会“研究”人类）；对蜱虫、病毒和秃鹫等“不可爱者”（unloved others）的研究；Heather Paxson对后巴斯德时代微生物培养物的研究；将石头、天气系统等纳入“生命”范畴的尝试；Caitlin Berrigan用自己感染丙肝病毒的血液喂养蒲公英的表演艺术；以及Jeffrey Bussolini在家中建立的“猫科动物研究中心”等。这些案例共同说明了多物种研究如何将理论付诸实践，探索着各种形式的生命与非生命之间的深刻关联。

第一部分：引言与领域界定 (Untitled Introduction)

本部分奠定了多物种研究的理论基石，强调所有生命都产生于并依赖于多物种社群。文章从协同进化和“共同生成”（cobecoming）的概念出发，指出生命不仅是静态的生态交换，更是深刻纠缠的历史过程，其中充满了符号互动与意义交换。在此基础上，作者将多物种研究定位为一个关键领域，旨在回应“人类世”背景下的多重危机，并重新思考人类在生命网络中的位置、政治责任与伦理义务。它通过与动物研究等领域对比，界定了自身更广阔的范围（超越动物，关注微生物、植物、真菌乃至无生命体）和对“复数性”（multiplicity）的关注，即关注将不同生命体塑造成形的复杂关系网络，而非孤立的个体。

  • 生存的基本单位是关系性的: 作者引用Gregory Bateson的观点，强调任何有机体的生存都离不开其所处的环境，生命无法孤立存在。
    All living beings emerge from and make their lives within multispecies communities. As Gregory Bateson put it, the fundamental unit of survival is the “organism-in-itsenvironment.”1
  • 超越静态生态学: 对早期生态学中能量循环图的批判性反思，指出有机体之间的关系是深层、纠缠的历史性存在。
    Beyond a static ecological exchange, like the energy circuits mapped by early ecologists,2 organisms are situated within deep, entangled histories.
  • 协同进化的“相互捕获”: 引用Isabelle Stengers的概念来说明物种间（如花与蜂）的协同进化关系，这是一种相互塑造和成就的过程。
    The intimate relationship between a ﬂower and its pollinating bee is one in which both forms of life are shaped and made possible through a shared heritage, an entanglement that Isabelle Stengers characterizes as “reciprocal capture.”3
  • 符号交换与意义网络: 强调“共同生成”过程涉及多样的符号交换，包括生化、姿态和语言等，世界是一个充满意义的交流矩阵。
    This cobecoming involves the exchange and emergence of meanings, immersion in webs of signiﬁcation that might be linguistic, gestural, biochemical, and more.4
  • 世界是充满符号的生命矩阵: 引用Donna Haraway的说法，将世界描述为一个充满“符号与奇迹”的活泼交流网络。
    ...the world is a lively communicative matrix woven through with “signs and wonders.”5
  • 重新思考遗传与发展: 引用进化与发育生物学的新思想，强调遗传（基因、表观、行为、文化）是更宏大发展过程的一部分，这构成了“共同生成”的基础。
    In recent years these insights have reﬁgured and broken down a long-assumed divide between the sciences of evolution and ontogeny, requiring scientists and allies to rethink inheritances (genetic, epigenetic, behavioral, and cultural) as part of larger developmental processes.6
  • 原住民思想的贡献: 指出多物种研究与原住民长期以来的关系性、能动性思维存在深刻共鸣。
    In taking up these questions scholars are also engaging with long histories of relational, agentic thinking from indigenous peoples.7
  • 人类本质是跨物种关系: 引用Anna Tsing的观点，强调人类的本质是在与其他物种的互动关系中形成的。
    As Anna Tsing notes, “Human nature [in all its myriad forms] is an interspecies relation.”8
  • 人类世的话语: 列举了对当前时代的多种命名，指出无论如何命名，我们都需要关注复杂的相互关系。
    There are many names for our current condition—Anthropocene, Capitalocene, Plantationocene, Chthulucene, White-supremacy-cene, and the list goes on9—but whatever it is called, what it seems to demand are detailed practices of attentiveness to the complex ways that we, all of us, become in consequential relationship with others.
  • 政治经济学的多物种维度: 探讨殖民主义、资本主义等不平等权力关系如何在更广泛的生命网络中展开。
    How do colonialism, capitalism, and their associated unequal power relations play out within a broader web of life?10
  • 后自然世界的保育问题: 在一个“后自然”的世界里，保育的定义和实践需要被重新思考。
    What will count as conservation in our postnatural world?11
  • 在“被摧毁的景观”中生活: 引用Anna Tsing的术语，探讨如何在被破坏的环境中形成新的社群并承担责任。
    What forms of responsibility are required, and how might we learn to respond in other, perhaps better ways to the communities taking form in “blasted landscapes”?12
  • 非人类研究者的可能性: 提及Deborah Bird Rose对澳洲原住民长者观点的转述，即蚂蚁也可能在对人类进行“研究”，挑战了研究主体的定义。
    Incidentally, the “researcher” need not always be human. For a short discussion of ants conducting research on sleeping people, see the views of Steve Meredith (an Aboriginal Australian Ngiyampaa elder) in Rose, “Val Plumwood’s Philosophical Animism,” 99.13
  • “自我”的生态学: 引用Eduardo Kohn的观点，将环境理解为由能动主体共同塑造的、充满意义、兴趣和情感的动态场域，而非静态背景。
    Rather, they are complex “ecologies of selves,”14 dynamic milieus that are continually shaped and reshaped, actively—even if not always knowingly—crafted through the sharing of “meanings, interests and affects,”15...
  • 拓展“生命”的边界: 指出多物种研究正将注意力扩展到传统意义上的“非生命”实体，如化学物种。
    The Multispecies Salon has lately begun to consider “chemical species” as a frame for exploring the unexpected possibilities...16
  • 宇宙-生态学路径: 提及Vinciane Despret和Michel Meuret的贡献，他们将神、祖先和灵魂等纳入生命形式的讨论中，扩展了世界的构成。
    For example, in their contribution to this collection, Vinciane Despret and Michel Meuret articulate a cosmo-ecological approach that brings gods, ancestors, and spirits into our accounts of the forms of life...17
  • 挑战生物中心主义: 指出一些学者正挑战多物种研究中潜在的“生物偏见”，认为生物中心主义本身可能是一种不合理的偏见。
    In this way, a growing group of scholars is challenging the biotic prejudices of multispecies work.18
  • 新唯物主义的贡献: 引用新唯物主义的观点，强调物质的活力，为讨论“非生命”的能动性提供理论基础。
    Grounded in important insights from a range of ﬁelds—including new materialisms,19 political geology,20 and indigenous metaphysics21—the liveliness of the abiotic is being brought to the fore.
  • 非生物体的动物行为学: 引用德勒兹的观点，认为地质构造、河流等非生命实体也可能有其独特的生命方式和纠缠历史，因此也可以成为动物行为学研究的对象。
    Many entities, from geologic formations and rivers to glaciers, might themselves be thought to have distinctive ways of life...that is, ways of affecting and being affected, and so they too might become subjects of ethologies in the Deleuzian sense of the term.22
  • “物种”概念的再思考: 引用Donna Haraway的观点，将“species”理解为更广泛的“亲属/种类”的集合，而非固定的生物学分类。
    The term species in multispecies studies gestures to particular ways of life and to any relevant gathering together of kin and/or kind (as Donna Haraway has argued, pointing to the historically much broader meanings of the term species).23
  • 分类行为的非人类中心性: 反驳了“物种”是人类中心强加概念的观点，指出分类和区分行为在非人类世界中普遍存在。
    While some cultural critics have suggested that the notion of species is an anthropocentric imposition on the world,25 close attention to other kinds of life reveals that humans are not exceptional in our ability to classify and categorize.
  • 核心方法论——激情沉浸: 总结多物种研究的共通点在于通过对非人类生活“充满激情的沉浸”来理解其伦理、政治和认识论意义。
    In other words, each of them is an example of the new science studies that Tsing has pointed to, one that is grounded in “passionate immersion in the lives of the nonhumans being studied.”32

第二部分：激情沉浸 (Passionate Immersion)

这一部分详细阐述了多物种研究的核心方法论——“充满激情的沉浸”（Passionate Immersion）。作者指出，这种沉浸要求研究者深入、细致地关注非人类的生命方式和经验世界，而非仅仅将其视为人类生活的符号或资源。这种“激情”并非指盲目热爱，也包括对令人不适或被厌恶的生物（如蜱虫、病毒）的关注。本节系统梳理了实现这种沉浸的多种路径：挖掘哲学、人类学等学科史中的前学科（pre-disciplinary）思想资源；批判性地阅读和重构当代自然科学文献，挑战其正统观念（如拟人论的禁忌）；重视和学习原住民、猎人、农民等群体的在地知识；与艺术家合作，通过表演和装置艺术等非传统形式进行探究；以及通过与伴侣物种的日常生活互动（准民族志）来产生新知识。最终，作者强调了跨学科合作的重要性，认为这是应对多物种世界复杂性的必然选择。

  • “厚描”非人类世界: 借用Geertz的术语，主张对其他物种独特的经验世界、存在方式和生物文化依恋进行“厚重”的描述。
    Beyond viewing other creatures as mere symbols, resources, or background for the lives of humans, scholars in multispecies studies have aimed to provide “thick” accounts of the distinctive experiential worlds, modes of being, and biocultural attachments of other species.34
  • 关注“笨拙”与“不可爱”的生物: 强调“激情沉浸”也包括对那些不讨喜、甚至令人厌恶的生物的深入研究。
    Immersion in the lives of the awkward, the unloved, or even the loathed is very possible.35 As such, some of this scholarship runs against the grain of dominant norms and sentiments, cultivating attentiveness to such creatures as ticks,36 pathogenic viruses,37 and vultures.38
  • 与“有益”生物共存的研究: 列举了与人类生活紧密相关且通常被视为有益的生物的研究，如Haraway的狗和Paxson的微生物。
    Other work, such as Haraway’s canine companions39 and Heather Paxson’s post-Pasteurian microbial cultures,40 has orbited around critters that are good for humans to live with.
  • 激情沉浸的核心: 定义“激情沉浸”为一种好奇和纠缠的过程，即“学会被影响”，从而以不同方式去理解和关怀。
    In short, passionate immersion means becoming curious and so entangled, “learning to be affected”42 and so perhaps to understand and care a little differently.
  • 挖掘历史档案中的智识盟友: 指出在“两种文化”分野固化之前，已有学者（如Lewis Henry Morgan）进行跨学科研究，为多物种研究提供了思想先例。
    For example, Lewis Henry Morgan’s 1868 The American Beaver and His Works was a study conducted before biology and anthropology were established as distinct disciplines.44
  • 哲学史中的多物种关注: 引用Goethe、Marder和Steiner的例子，说明哲学传统中也存在对植物和动物生命方式的细致关注。
    Goethe’s 1790 The Metamorphosis of Plants stands out as an early example of observational rigor coming together with theoretical imagination... Michael Marder has worked through the archives of European philosophy...46 Gary Steiner has done something similar for animal minds.47
  • 批判性重读科学文献: 以Haraway和Despret为例，展示了如何通过批判性地解读动物行为学和生物学文献来重构对其他物种世界的理解。
    Haraway’s work on primates stands out as an early example of this approach... Others, such as Belgian philosopher Despret, have developed extensive bodies of related work...49
  • 挑战竞争性个人主义的生物学叙事: Hustak和Myers的研究通过关注植物的智慧和能动性，反思了从达尔文到新达尔文主义中占主导地位的竞争性个人主义。
    In a similar vein, Carla Hustak and Natasha Myers have explored the intelligence and agency of plants, rethinking the centrality of competitive individualism in dominant biological accounts...51
  • 挑战“拟人论”禁忌: 引用Val Plumwood的观点，认为“拟人论”的指责常常扼杀而非开启关于人与非人共享特质的批判性探究。
    The charge of anthropomorphism shuts down discussion, according to Val Plumwood, rather than opening up critical inquiry about how elements of a given trait may or may not be shared by nonhumans.55
  • 反思“机械拟形论”与“拟人否定论”: 引用Eileen Crist和Frans de Waal的观点，指出避免拟人论的努力本身也可能陷入将生命机械化或否认与人类共通性的陷阱。
    At the same time, as Eileen Crist has argued, efforts to adopt neutral language have themselves often been mechanomorphic... or exhibited entrenched forms of what de Waal has called “anthropodenial.”56
  • 轶事的价值: 认为田野观察中的轶事可以成为宝贵资源，帮助我们认识到物种内部的个体多样性和创造力。
    As some ethologists have noted, anecdote can be a remarkable resource,57 allowing us to move outside a narrow space of species-typical behaviors to recognize individual or social diversity and creative capacity...
  • 知识的去殖民化: 强调多物种研究致力于挑战科学知识的霸权，寻求和尊重其他知识体系，如原住民、猎人、农民的知识。
    ...the ﬁeld has also sought out a range of other approaches, aiming to decolonize58 and more broadly challenge dominant assumptions about knowledge, expertise, and who is authorized to speak for Nature.
  • 艺术家的参与: 指出艺术家通过多媒体装置和表演性干预，成为挑战传统自然代言方式的核心参与者。
    Artists have also become core participants in scholarly projects that question conventional approaches to speaking for Nature, exploring opportunities for immersion in the lives of others.62
  • 表演性实验与思辨未来: 艺术家和民族志学者通过表演性实验来探索多物种世界的思辨维度，思考生命可能成为的样子。
    Performative experiments are also being used by artists and ethnographers to probe speculative dimensions of multispecies worlds... scholars in the ﬁeld of multispecies studies are engaging with people in their speculations about what life might or could be.64
  • 伴侣物种与准民族志: 个人与伴侣动物或植物的相遇，成为一种经验知识的丰富来源，重塑了“实验室”和“田野”的概念。
    Personal encounters with companion critters—some of which are commonly called pets and house plants—have also given rise to a rich corpus of empirical knowledge...67
  • 家庭作为实验室: 以Jeffrey Bussolini和Ananya Mukherjea的“猫科动物研究中心”为例，展示了非正式实验空间如何产生传统实验室无法提供的细致观察。
    At the Centre for Feline Studies, Jeffrey Bussolini and Ananya Mukherjea have established a new kind of laboratory with six cats in their Manhattan apartment.69
  • 跨学科协作的必要性: 认为沉浸于多物种世界需要组建合作团队，汇集互补的技能和专业知识，打破传统人文学科的单打独斗模式。
    Immersing oneself in multispecies worlds often necessitates forming collaborative teams to bring together complimentary skills and expertise.

第三部分：多物种世界建构 (Multispecies Worldings)

本部分深入探讨了多物种研究的理论核心和伦理政治意涵。作者引入“世界建构”（worlding）的概念，拒绝了实在论与相对主义的二元对立，主张世界是在物质-符号的相互作用中被共同构成的。借鉴Karen Barad的“能动实在论”（agential realism），作者强调认识（epistemology）与存在（ontology）不可分割，我们的认知方式和实践直接参与塑造现实世界。这一立场旨在解构自然/文化、人类/自然的二元论，但并非要抹平差异，而是要“增加对差异的关注”。文章接着将这一理论应用于对“人类”自身的重构，指出从微生物组到文化实践，人类的存在本身就是多物种的集合体。然而，这种纠缠并非均质的，作者强调必须关注其中不平等的劳动、风险和权力关系。最终，文章将讨论引向伦理层面，提出一种关系性的伦理学，它拒绝普适原则，主张“与麻烦共存”（staying with the trouble），在具体的、充满矛盾的情境中反复协商和构建“好”的生活方式。这种伦理学的核心就是“关注的艺术”（arts of attentiveness）——它既是认识他者的实践，也是学习如何更好地回应他者、共同创造更繁荣世界的技艺。

  • 世界建构与能动实在论: 拒绝实在论与相对主义的二元对立，通过Barad的“能动实在论”强调世界是在物质-符号的相互作用中被共同构成的。
    Refusing the choice between unreconstructed realism and an easy relativism...the notion of worlding insists on the coconstitution, the material-semiotic interplay, that shapes what is.82 There is a particular variety of realism—what Karen Barad has called an “agential realism”83—at play here...
  • 认识论与本体论的融合: 强调认识世界的方式和实践本身就是世界构成的一部分，两者不可分割。
    From this perspective, any absolute division between epistemology and ontology breaks down as worlds emerge and are continually reshaped through dynamic intra-actions.84
  • 社会的构建过程: 引用Latour的观点，指出“社会”不是构建世界的材料，而是指不同能动者共同组装、塑造结果的过程。
    As Latour reminds us, the social is not the stuff or material of this construction; rather, it names the process of assembly in which diverse agencies each exert their own force in the shaping of outcomes.85
  • 解构自然/文化二元论: 多物种研究旨在批判性地重构“人类”，并跨越自然/文化、自然/人类的二元对立。
    ...work in multispecies studies joins other scholarship— traveling under such names as nature-cultures and posthumanism—that aims to critically reﬁgure the human while problematizing and working across nature/culture and nature/human dualisms.89
  • 关注差异而非抹平差异: 引用Mick Smith对Derrida的解读，强调解构二元论的重点是“增加对各层次差异的关注”，而非简单地将范畴合并。
    As Mick Smith notes...this scholarship “recognises ‘the fragility and porosity of the limit between nature and culture’ not so as to collapse these categories into each other...but to ‘multiply attention to differences’ at all levels”90...
  • 人类作为多物种集合体: 从微生物组的角度出发，揭示每个人的身体都是一个由寄生和共生生命形式构成的、复杂的物种复合物。
    ...emerging ﬁndings about the microbiome reveal that, within our bodies and stretching out well beyond them, each of us is a species multiple: each of our bodies is crowded with diverse kinds of parasitic and symbiotic forms of life.101
  • 身体是多重性的集合: 引用Annemarie Mol和Deleuze & Guattari的观点，将身体描述为“一个精心协调的人群”和“多重性的多重性”。
    This work gives a new twist to Annemarie Mol’s claim that a body is “an intricately coordinated crowd.”102 In the words of Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari, bodies have become “multiplicities of multiplicities...”103
  • 人类文化的动物起源: 引用Lestel和Rugemer的观点，认为人类文化并非脱离自然的产物，而是对自然某个极端生态位的探索。
    From this perspective, human cultures are not departures, or outgrowths, from a more fundamental biologic nature but are, rather, another expression of that nature...105
  • 不平等的纠缠: 强调多物种纠缠中存在的权力不平等、风险不均和差异化的位置性，必须进行细致的历史和民族志考察。
    Histories of gender and race, of political economy and colonization are layered into multispecies worldings—how could they not be?—shaping possibilities for everyone.109
  • 关系性伦理与责任: 提出一种根植于我们作为世界共构者一部分的伦理观，核心是为我们所处的生成性关系负责。
    As Barad puts it, “Ethics is therefore not about right response to a radically exterior/ized other, but about responsibility and accountability for the lively relationalities of becoming of which we are a part.”112
  • 拒绝普适伦理原则: 主张一种关系性的伦理方法，拒绝通过诉诸不容置疑的原则或专家意见来终结政治过程，而是“与麻烦共存”。
    Staying with the trouble, it aims to hold onto competing ethical obligations, multiplying perspectives on what counts as “the good.” There are no neat and ﬁnal answers here, nor are there any trump cards that shut down the political process...114
  • 故事作为世界建构的技术: 引用Haraway的观点，指出我们讲述的故事是塑造生活方式的能动技术，具有伦理和政治力量。
    Like all other accounts, multispecies stories are active technologies of worlding: “Stories are means to ways of living.”118
  • Cui bono? (谁受益?): 提出关键的政治问题，即在物种相遇时，谁会从中受益，从而超越对多物种混杂的简单庆祝。
    ...this work also engages with the more analytically interesting and politically charged questions that follow from asking cui bono: who beneﬁts when species meet?119
  • “关注的艺术”的双重含义: 将“attentiveness”定义为两部分：一是认识他者特殊性的实践，二是学习如何更好地回应他者、促进共同繁荣的实践。
    This attentiveness is a two-part proposition: both a practice of getting to know another in their intimate particularity...and, at the same time, a practice of learning how one might better respond to another...
